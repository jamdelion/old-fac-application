Entrepreneur

Straight after university, I wanted to start a business that would make a difference in my community…
See more
...so I started my own vegan pop-up café company!
I wanted to change the perception that veganism was a flavourless lentil hell, to encourage people to make dietary choices that were moreethicalandenvironmentally sustainable.
What better way than by offering delicious, unpretentious, plant-powered junk food?
I also wanted to make vegan food accessible. At every pop-up, I made one or two menu itemspay-as-you-feelitems.
My customers always had the option of something delicious that was good for the planet, at whatever price they could afford.
My long-term plan was to make this Cambridge’s first vegan, zero-waste workers’ cooperative cafe! (Sadly not meant to be!)
This project led to me getting my current day job, as a worker-member ofArjuna Wholefoods,a vegetarian workers’ cooperative shop in Cambridge.

Coder

I have only just started on my coding journey, but I have so much further to go…
See more
I first got into coding out of a linguist's curiosity to find out what the weird symbols on my housemate's laptop screen meant. I didn't anticipate that before long, I'd be dreaming about closing brackets, and obsessively inspecting cool-looking websites for ideas!
I realised that turning coding into a career could easily be a way for me to satisfy my entrepreneurial itches, my intellectual curiosity and equip me with skills that could be used to actually make a difference in the world.
I started trying to teach myself to code more seriously, first completing Coursera's"Learn to Program: The Fundamentals"and attending aDjangoGirlsworkshop.
After coming across F&C when looking for more free courses to complete, I decided to use the pre-requisites as the basis for my coding self-education. My daily routine soon incorporated lessons on FreeCodeCamp and a challenge or two on CodeWars, and my love for coding only grew stronger!

Why FAC?

Values

  • The values of F&C strongly align with my own.
  • I aim to define my career by making a difference to the world, so having the opportunity to learn to code while solving worthwhile problems that actually help people is a great motivation for me.
  • As a non-profit and by providing free education, F&C embodies a socialist ideal - the right to learn.

Community

  • I can't wait to dive into a learning environment where I am supported by like-minded people who share my enthusiasm and commitment to the aims of the course.
  • alumni community, nobody gets left behind

Cooperative

  • As a self-proclaimed coops geek, I am totally into collective decision-making, inter-cooperative support etc.
  • monthly membership meetings of alum community, AGMs where priorities collectively set (lifelong member of community), member of CoTech network

Diversity

  • keen for an inclusive organisation, learn new ways of thinking from being in a group where diversity of experiences represented.
  • Safe space, women taken seriously etc

Career

  • serious about making a career out of becoming a dev, enviable set of company links.
  • full-time, focused
  • journey of a lifetime, this changes everything
  • workspaces that seem dynamic, exciting, driven to making the world a better place, flexible etc
